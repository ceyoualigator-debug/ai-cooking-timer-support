AI Cooking Timer

Support

Need help with photo timers, notifications, widgets, language settings, Siri, Shortcuts, or Premium? Start here.

Basic use

Open the app, tap the plus button, take a cooking photo, set the time, and start the timer. The photo becomes the timer card, so no label is required. When several timers are running, the app sorts them by least time remaining and shows the most urgent timer first.

Notifications

If timer alerts do not appear, make sure notifications, sounds, and Time Sensitive alerts are enabled for AI Cooking Timer in iOS Settings. The app uses local notifications, so no account or server is required.

Finished timer actions

When a timer finishes, you can open the app from the notification, stop the timer, or add one more minute when the action is available.

Widgets and Live Activity

Add the AI Cooking Timer widget from the Home Screen widget gallery to keep the next timer visible outside the app. On supported iOS versions, Live Activity can also show the next running timer on system surfaces.

Siri and Shortcuts

Use Siri or the Shortcuts app to start a cooking timer, add one minute to the next timer, stop the current timer, or show the next timer when supported by your device language and iOS version.

Language

AI Cooking Timer follows your device language by default. You can also choose a preferred app language in the app settings.

Timer suggestions

The app can suggest a duration when a new cooking photo looks similar to previous timers. Suggestions are learned on device and can be cleared in Settings.

Premium

The free version supports up to two active timers. AI Cooking Timer Premium is a one-time purchase that unlocks unlimited active timers. Purchases and restores are handled by Apple.

Contact support

Open a support request at GitHub Issues. Include your device model, iOS version, and what you were doing when the issue happened.
